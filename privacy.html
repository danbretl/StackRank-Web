Plain-language policy

Privacy

StackRank is designed to work without an account. Your movie ranking stays in this browser unless you choose to sign in and sync it.

Effective June 29, 2026

What is stored

While signed out, StackRank stores your ranking, watch and skip queues, pack progress, and Share Studio preferences in your browser’s local storage. This data does not leave your device except when a feature needs movie data or you intentionally download, share, or import something.

If you sign in, Supabase stores your account identity and syncs your ranking, queues, and pack progress so they are available on your other devices. StackRank does not receive or store a password.

Services the app contacts

  • TMDB supplies movie search results, details, suggestions, and artwork. Those requests are proxied through StackRank’s Supabase functions.
  • Supabase provides optional account sign-in, cross-device sync, server functions, and privacy-bounded product events.
  • Vercel hosts the site and provides aggregate Web Analytics pageviews.
  • Google Fonts and jsDelivr deliver the site font and the Supabase browser library.

Like most internet services, these providers may receive normal connection metadata such as an IP address and browser information when your device contacts them. Their handling of that data is governed by their own policies.

Product measurement

StackRank measures whether core flows work and get used. Custom product events use a temporary per-page identifier, accept only an allowlist of coarse properties, and never include movie titles, movie IDs, email addresses, or account IDs. StackRank also uses Vercel Web Analytics for aggregate pageviews.

“Do Not Track” and Global Privacy Control signals disable StackRank’s custom product events. StackRank does not sell personal information, run behavioral advertising, or use advertising cookies.

Your controls

You can download a full backup, clear your ranking, restore or import a list, and sign out from the settings menu. Clearing browser storage removes signed-out data from that browser. To request deletion of a signed-in account and its server data, email stackrank@danbretl.com.

Ranking and account data is kept while you use the service or until it is deleted. Limited operational and product-measurement records are retained only as needed to operate and evaluate StackRank.
